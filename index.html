Accuracy and Bias in Perceiving Romantic Partners' Emotions

Background

Generally, people assume that they accurately perceive their partners. We like to think that we know when our partners are happy and when they are upset. However, it's likely that in some instances, our motives skew our perspectives, leading us to see partners through tinted glasses, resulting in biases in our perceptions.

Research Question

My dissertation explored the extent to which people were accurate and or biased in perceiving their romantic partners' emotional experiences. I further examined how these accurate and biased perceptions affected couples' relationship satisfaction the following day and five years later.

Method

Using experience sampling methods (i.e., daily diary), a longitudinal follow-up, and dyadic truth and bias models, I observed both the short- and long-term effects of accurate and biased partner perceptions.

Findings

This work showed that people are both accurate and biased when perceiving their partners' daily emotions, and these perceptions each meaningfully shape relationship satisfaction.

  • Accurately recognizing negative emotions predicted greater next-day partner satisfaction, whereas underestimating (i.e., bias) negative emotions predicted lower satisfaction.
  • Interestingly, underestimating positive emotions was linked to higher partner satisfaction the next day and five years later.

Empathic Effort and Interpersonal Accuracy as Mechanisms that Encourage Emotion Expression

Background

Existing research suggests that people are more emotionally open with communal partners because they anticipate support rather than rejection or exploitation. However, the mechanisms driving emotional expression in these close relationships remain unclear. Thus, this study examined two possible explanations.

Research Question

I examined whether people are more emotionally open in communal relationships because they perceive greater empathic effort (i.e., communal partners try harder to understand their feelings), greater interpersonal accuracy (i.e., communal partners understand their feelings more accurately), or both.

Method

This work employed a cross-sectional design. Psychometric scales were developed to measure empathic effort and interpersonal accuracy. Multilevel SEM models were used to test effort and accuracy as mediators.

Findings

Both empathic effort and interpersonal accuracy influence our likelihood of opening up to communal partners.

  • Perceiving that a partner understands one well (i.e., interpersonal accuracy) is a stronger predictor of expression with a partner, compared to perceived effort.
  • However, people are still more likely to express emotions with partners whom they perceive put in more effort.

Daily Emotion Regulation in Romantic Relationships Before and During COVID

Background

While research shows that individuals can regulate their own emotions, less is known about how romantic partners regulate each other's emotions.

Research Question

This study explored the emotion regulation strategies partners use to influence each other's emotions, evaluated their effectiveness from both the target's and regulator's perspectives, and tested whether a life-changing stressor like COVID-19 moderated these effects.

Method

In a two-week daily diary study, we had 197 cohabiting couples report on their efforts to regulate each other's positive and negative emotions, with data collected before and during pandemic stay-at-home restrictions. Psychometric scales were developed for this work and multilevel moderation models were used.

Findings

Generally, emotion regulation efforts were perceived as similarly effective before and during COVID-19.

  • During COVID-19, partners reported using more suppression to manage negative emotions, and this increase was also perceived by their partners.
  • Before the pandemic, greater use of suppression was linked to higher perceived effectiveness among women, but this pattern did not hold during COVID-19, suggesting that the impact of suppression depends on context.

Who Values Empathic Effort and Interpersonal Accuracy in Close Relationships?

Background

Prior research shows that relationship satisfaction is higher when individuals perceive their partners as accurate and effortful in trying to understand them. However, it remains unclear whether these perceptions are especially important in closer relationships compared to more distant ones.

Research Question

This study examined whether communally oriented individuals, those who tend to express greater care and concern for others, are more satisfied with a partner's empathic effort and interpersonal accuracy.

Method

Perceived empathic effort and interpersonal accuracy from a friend were experimentally manipulated to assess their impact on relationship satisfaction. Psychometric scales were developed for this study to theoretically and statistically differentiate between effort and accuracy.

Findings

Individuals with a stronger communal orientation showed greater appreciation for empathic behavior, likely because they interpreted effort and accuracy as reciprocated care.

  • Even when partners understood accurately, perceived effort still mattered. People felt more satisfied when they believed their partner genuinely tried to understand them.
  • The most satisfying interactions occurred when accurate understanding was paired with visible effort.

Designing a Centralized User Research Repository

Overview

Contributed to the design and rollout of a centralized, GDPR-compliant User Research Repository (URR) to improve access to user research across departments.

Problem

User research was siloed across teams, leading to duplicated efforts, limited cross-team insights, and missed opportunities for cross-product alignment.

My Role

  • Organized repository structure and information architecture to improve discoverability
  • Developed documentation and best-practice usage guidelines
  • Led and supported town hall demonstrations to introduce the URR
  • Helped onboard teams and drive cross-department adoption

Impact

Increased visibility of user insights across products, reduced redundant research efforts, enabled more user-centered, data-informed decision-making, and supported AI-assisted analyses for faster insight generation.

Uncovering Key UX Insights from Voice of the Customer Data

Overview

Conducted qualitative analysis of 2022–2023 Voice of the Customer survey data to uncover recurring patterns in customer sentiment and identify systemic UX concerns.

Problem

Customers consistently expressed feeling unheard. Key themes emerged around:

  • UI inconsistency and disruptive product changes
  • Usability gaps compared to competitors
  • Insufficient training and multilingual documentation
  • Pricing transparency, licensing flexibility, and trust
  • Technical support responsiveness and software stability

My Role

  • Analyzed large volumes of open-ended survey responses
  • Conducted thematic coding and grouped insights into core pain points
  • Extracted representative quotes to ground findings in user voice
  • Synthesized research into clear, evidence-backed recommendations

Impact

Transformed raw customer feedback into structured insights that highlighted systemic UX, trust, and ecosystem-level challenges. This helped stakeholders prioritize more user-centered design practices and improved communication strategies.

Synthesizing User Insights from Social Media Feedback

Overview

Analyzed 18 months of social media data using Sprinklr to extract genuine customer feedback and market perceptions. Synthesized insights to inform an internal UX initiative and establish a repeatable process for ongoing social listening across products.

Problem

The company lacked a structured approach to capture social sentiment, making it difficult to:

  • Identify recurring customer concerns and discussions
  • Understand public perception of products
  • Translate unstructured social feedback into actionable insights for design improvements

My Role

  • Filtered and analyzed social media data using Sprinklr
  • Conducted qualitative synthesis to identify recurring themes and sentiment trends
  • Designed a repeatable workflow for future social listening efforts

Impact

Delivered structured, evidence-backed insights that informed UX strategy, highlighted customer pain points and sentiment trends, and established a process for continuous integration of social feedback into product and design decisions.

Analyzing Long-Term Customer Perceptions for UX Strategy

Overview

Conducted a longitudinal analysis of Voice of the Customer survey data (2017–2023) to uncover trends in user satisfaction, usage rates, product value, loyalty, and likelihood to renew or recommend.

Problem

Stakeholders lacked insight into long-term trends and drivers of customer loyalty. They needed to understand:

  • Changes in satisfaction, renewal, and recommendation over time
  • How product usage and contract type influence customer outcomes
  • Emerging priorities and competitor comparisons

My Role

  • Compiled and analyzed multi-year survey data to detect statistically significant trends
  • Examined key metrics, including satisfaction, renewal likelihood, and recommendation patterns
  • Synthesized findings into actionable insights for stakeholders

Impact

Highlighted shifts in satisfaction, loyalty, and recommendation trends, answered strategic questions to inform product strategy and UX improvements, and provided evidence-based recommendations combining quantitative and qualitative insights.
